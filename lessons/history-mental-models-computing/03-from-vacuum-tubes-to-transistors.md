# Lesson 03: From Vacuum Tubes to Transistors

> **The one thing to remember**: Computing did not become modern just because of better ideas.
> It also needed better physical components. The move from vacuum tubes to transistors transformed reliability, size, power use, and scalability.

---

## Start With a Machine That Is Too Large and Too Fragile

Early electronic computers were impressive, but they faced practical problems:

- they were large
- they consumed significant power
- they produced substantial heat
- they were less reliable than later designs

That meant the path to mass practical computing required a major hardware shift.

---

## Vacuum Tubes

Vacuum tubes made early electronic switching possible, which was a huge step beyond purely mechanical systems.

But they came with major costs:

- size
- heat
- fragility
- power consumption

They proved that electronic computing could work, but they were not ideal for scaling computing into the world we know today.

---

## Transistors Changed the Equation

Transistors provided a much better switching component for large-scale digital systems.

At a high level, they enabled machines that were:

- smaller
- more reliable
- less power-hungry
- more scalable

This was one of the great physical turning points in computing history.

---

## Why Scaling Matters

Better switching components did not just make machines cheaper or smaller.
They made it possible to build much more complex systems with many more logic elements.

That matters because modern computing power is deeply tied to how many useful switching elements can be manufactured, connected, and coordinated effectively.

This is a major precursor to later ideas like Moore's Law and integrated circuits.

---

## Why Developers Should Care

This transition explains:

- why physical device technology matters to software history too
- why modern computing could scale in complexity and availability
- why power, size, and reliability constraints are not new topics but long-running design forces

Without this transition, much of modern software culture would never have had the hardware foundation it needed.

---

## Recap

- Early electronic computers relied on vacuum tubes.
- Vacuum tubes made electronic computing possible but scaled poorly.
- Transistors enabled smaller, more reliable, more scalable systems.
- Hardware component advances were essential to the practical growth of modern computing.
