# Lesson 65: What's Next — Beyond This Track

> **Analogy**: Finishing a long course is like finishing basic flight
> training. You are no longer trying to figure out what the instruments
> do. Now the real growth comes from hours in the air: varied conditions,
> more difficult routes, and sharper judgment. This lesson is about how
> to keep flying upward after the track ends.

---

## Why This Matters

The point of this track was not to make you memorize a closed list of
patterns. It was to give you a durable problem-solving foundation.

After finishing, your next challenge is not “learn 500 more tricks.”
It is to convert knowledge into fluency.

That means:

- sustained practice
- deliberate review of weak spots
- exposure to contest-style and system-level problems
- gradual entry into more advanced topics

---

## A Practical Next-Step Plan

### Stage 1: Consolidate What You Learned

For the next 4 to 6 weeks:

- revisit your weakest pattern families
- redo selected medium and hard problems without notes
- explain solutions out loud before coding
- keep a mistake log of wrong pattern choices and missed invariants

### Recommended rhythm

```
  2 easy/medium review problems
  2 medium problems from weak areas
  1 hard problem with full write-up
  1 session for reviewing mistakes only
```

Consistency matters more than extreme daily volume.

## How To Keep Improving

### 1. Build a weakness map

Track mistakes by category:

- wrong pattern selection
- correct pattern, wrong invariant
- DP state definition mistake
- graph-state modeling mistake
- implementation bug after correct idea

That tells you what kind of training you actually need.

### 2. Re-solve, do not only read

Reading editorials helps, but fluency grows when you reconstruct the
solution yourself after a delay.

### 3. Mix depth and breadth

Do not solve only new problems.
Alternate between:

- new exposure
- spaced repetition on past mistakes

### 4. Practice explanation, not only implementation

In interviews, the explanation often matters as much as the final code.
Train yourself to say:

- the state
- the invariant
- the bottleneck
- the complexity

clearly and quickly.

## Suggested Weekly Practice Schedule

```
  Monday     pattern review + 2 medium problems
  Tuesday    1 hard problem
  Wednesday  weak-area review + note cleanup
  Thursday   2 medium / 1 hard mixed
  Friday     mock interview or timed set
  Saturday   contest / long-form hard problem
  Sunday     rest or light editorial review
```

Adjust the volume to your schedule, but keep the review loop.

---

## How To Know You Are Improving

Look for these signals:

- you reject bad approaches faster
- you identify the right state sooner
- you make fewer invariant mistakes
- you can explain the solution before coding it
- hard problems feel difficult but not shapeless

Those are better indicators than raw problem count.
